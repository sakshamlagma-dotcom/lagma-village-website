About Lagma

A village of heritage, learning, and togetherness

Lagma carries the quiet strength of rural Bihar: fertile land, shared festivals, deep family values, and a strong desire for progress.

Village Identity

Lagma is a historic village in Saharsa district, Bihar. The village is known for agriculture, ponds, temples, orchards, bamboo clusters, and a community life built on cooperation and respect.

People in Lagma commonly speak Maithili and Hindi. Maithili remains central to everyday conversation, songs, family ceremonies, and the emotional identity of the region.

Agriculture and Ponds

Farming is an important part of village life. Paddy, wheat, maize, mustard, and seasonal vegetables are cultivated in and around the village. Ponds support fish farming, irrigation, groundwater recharge, and the ecological balance of the area.

Culture and Festivals

Festivals bring families and neighbors together. Homes are cleaned and decorated, special food is prepared, devotional music is played, and relatives return to the village to celebrate with their families.

Temples and Brahm Sthan hold an important place in spiritual life, while folk songs and traditional customs continue to connect younger generations with their roots.

Education and Aspirations

Education has shaped Lagma's progress for decades. The village school, established in 1956, has helped many students build careers in government service, business, technology, and other fields.

Young people increasingly pursue studies and work outside the village, but many continue to support local development and remain connected to their ancestral home.

Governance and Facilities

Local governance is supported through the Panchayat system. The village has access to education, electricity, roads, mobile connectivity, irrigation facilities, and nearby banking and health services.

Vision for the Future

Lagma's future depends on balancing tradition with progress. Better education, digital access, sustainable farming, healthcare, and infrastructure can help the village grow while preserving its cultural identity.
